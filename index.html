🚨 Wire Fraud / Phishing Response Protocol

⚠️ Wire fraud is the #1 threat in real estate closings. If a customer reports sending wired funds to a fraudulent account or receiving suspicious wire instruction emails — treat this as a CRITICAL EMERGENCY. Every minute counts.
Step 1: Stay calm — reassure the customer you are taking immediate action.
Step 2: Ask: When was the wire sent? How much? To which bank? Get wire reference number if available.
Step 3: Immediately contact First American's Fraud Team: 1-800-854-3643 — do not wait.
Step 4: Advise customer to call their bank immediately to attempt a wire recall — time is critical (window is typically under 24 hours).
Step 5: Advise customer to file a complaint at the FBI's IC3: ic3.gov
Step 6: Log full details in CRM — flag ticket as FRAUD / URGENT. Note all parties, amounts, and timeline.
Step 7: Do NOT send or confirm any new wire instructions until fraud investigation is complete and parties are re-verified by phone.

🛡️ Wire Fraud Prevention — Customer Education

Red Flags to Share with Customers:
— Last-minute changes to wire instructions via email
— Urgency pressure ("must wire today or deal dies")
— Email addresses that look similar but aren't exact
— Requests to keep the wire "confidential"
— Unknown third parties claiming to handle funds

First American's Safe Practices:
— Always call a known number to verify wire instructions
— Use the Transactions Portal for secure communications
— Never wire based solely on emailed instructions
— Enroll in First American's free title monitoring
— Visit firstam.com/fraud-protection for resources

🪪 Identity Theft & Phishing Protocol

If customer received a suspicious email claiming to be from First American — do NOT click any links. Forward to phishing@firstam.com.
Verify caller identity using full legal name + property address + last 4 of SSN if accessing sensitive order data.
If customer suspects their identity was used to open a fraudulent title order — escalate to Fraud Team immediately.
Send customer link to First American's Security & Privacy Center: firstam.com/security-and-privacy.
Log all details including suspicious email headers, phone numbers, or website URLs mentioned by the customer.

📞 Customer Call Workflow

Open: "Thank you for calling First American. My name is [Name] — how can I assist you today?"
Fraud Screen: If customer mentions a wire, funds, or unusual email — immediately route to Fraud Protocol tab before anything else.
Verify Identity: Confirm full legal name + property address OR order number before accessing any account data.
Identify Role: Confirm whether the caller is buyer, seller, agent, lender, or attorney — context changes what information can be shared.
Listen Fully: Let the customer describe their issue completely before responding. Note key details: order numbers, dates, amounts, parties.
Confirm: Repeat the issue back: "So what I understand is [summary] — is that correct?"
Review: Pull up the order in the relevant system (AgentNet®, ClarityFirst®, IgniteRE™, or Home Warranty portal).
Update Closing Tracker: Confirm and update the closing progress steps in the Transaction tab.
Resolve or Escalate: Address the issue directly or escalate to the assigned title officer, claims department, or fraud team as appropriate.
Wire Reminder (every call): "As a reminder — never wire funds based on emailed instructions alone. Always call us at a known number to verify before sending any funds."
Additional Needs: "Is there anything else I can help you with today regarding your transaction or any First American services?"
Log Call: Document all interaction details, resolution, parties, and follow-up items in CRM system.
Close: "Thank you for choosing First American. We're proud to help protect one of your most important investments."
