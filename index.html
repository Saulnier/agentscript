🚨 Gas Leak / Emergency Protocol

⚠️ If customer reports smelling gas, a hissing sound, or dead vegetation in yard: Follow ALL steps below immediately. Do not troubleshoot — escalate to emergency.
Step 1: Tell customer to stop what they're doing and leave the building/area immediately — do not use any switches, phones, or appliances inside.
Step 2: Tell customer to go to a neighbor's house or a safe distance away before calling anyone.
Step 3: Direct customer to call 911 first.
Step 4: Then call Southwest Gas 24/7 Emergency: 877-860-6020 — dispatching a tech immediately.
Step 5: Do NOT re-enter the building until cleared by emergency responders and Southwest Gas technician.
Step 6: Log emergency dispatch in CRM with timestamp and customer address.

🔍 Gas Smell — Recognition Guide (For Agent Reference)

Natural Gas Smell: Rotten egg / sulfur odor (odorant added for detection)
Hissing Sound: Near appliance, meter, or pipe = possible leak
Dead Vegetation: Unexplained dying plants in a line = underground leak
Bubbling Water: In puddles or wet areas = underground leak

Do NOT:
— Turn lights or appliances on/off
— Use a landline phone inside
— Start a vehicle in the garage
— Smoke or use open flames
— Try to locate the leak yourself

🪪 Scam / Impersonator Protocol

Southwest Gas employees will always carry ID, wear uniforms, and drive marked vehicles. They will never ask for payment in the field or request personal info (card numbers, PINs, SSN) over the phone.

Advise customer to ask for employee ID and call 877-860-6020 to verify before allowing access.
If customer received a suspicious call or visit, document details and escalate to Fraud team.
Send scam awareness resources to customer email.

📞 Customer Call Workflow

Open: "Thank you for calling Southwest Gas, my name is [Name]. How can I help you today?"
Safety Check: If customer mentions gas smell or emergency — immediately switch to Safety Protocol tab.
Verify Identity: Confirm full name + service address OR account number before accessing account.
Listen: Allow customer to fully describe their issue without interrupting — note key details.
Confirm: Repeat the issue back: "So what I'm hearing is [summary] — is that correct?"
Review Account: Check account status, billing balance, service address, and relevant history.
Resolve or Escalate: Address the issue using available tools, or create a field tech dispatch / escalation ticket.
Assistance Check: If customer mentions bill hardship, proactively mention Energy Share, flexible payment plans, and 2-1-1 assistance.
Self-Service Reminder: Mention MyAccount portal and mobile app for payments, paperless billing, and service requests.
Confirm Resolution: "Is there anything else I can help you with today?" — confirm issue is addressed.
Log Call: Document all details, resolution, and any follow-up needed in CRM.
Close: "Thank you for calling Southwest Gas. We appreciate your business — stay warm and safe!"
